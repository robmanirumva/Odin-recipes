Rice

There are three basic kinds of rice: short-grain, long-grain, and medium-grain. Among them you'll
find hundreds of different varieties. Short-grain riceis rounded and plump, with a high starch content
that makes the grains stick together when cooked, especially if the rice has been milled to make
white rice.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Long-Grain White Rice
  • Onion Powder (or dried minced onion)
  • Black Pepper (ground)
  • Fresh Cilantro (chopped)

Steps

  1. Heat butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the rice and gently stir until rice begins to lightly brown.
  2. Add garlic, onion powder, and turmeric and stir until well mixed in.
  3. Slowly stir in chicken broth and raise heat to medium-high. Add salt and pepper to taste.
    Bring the mix to a boil then turn the heat to low and cover the pan with a lid.
  4. Remove from heat, add cilantro, and fluff with a fork. Serves 4.
